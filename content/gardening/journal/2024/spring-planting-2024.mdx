The weather has finally warmed up enough to start planting outdoors! This week I transplanted several seedlings that have been growing under lights since February.

## What I Planted

- **Tomatoes**: Roma and Cherry varieties, placed in the sunny south bed
- **Peppers**: Bell peppers and jalapeños near the tomatoes
- **Zucchini**: Three plants along the fence
- **Herbs**: Basil, oregano, and thyme in containers on the patio

## Observations

The soil temperature has consistently been above 15°C (60°F) for the past two weeks, which should be warm enough for the warm-season crops. I added compost and a balanced organic fertilizer before transplanting.

## Next Steps

- Install tomato cages before the plants get too large
- Set up drip irrigation for the main bed
- Start succession planting of lettuce and radishes
