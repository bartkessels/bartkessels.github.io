Jalapeños are the backbone of my hot pepper collection. Reliable, prolific, and versatile — they work fresh, pickled, smoked (chipotles), or roasted.

## Varieties I Grow

### Classic Jalapeño M
The standard supermarket jalapeño. Medium heat (2,500–8,000 SHU), thick walls, easy to grow.

### Mucho Nacho
A larger variety with slightly hotter fruit. Great for poppers and stuffing due to the bigger cavity.

## Growing Tips

1. **Heat for Germination**: Use a heat mat — jalapeño seeds germinate best at 27–32°C (80–90°F)
2. **Pinch Early Flowers**: Removing the first few flowers redirects energy into plant development
3. **Consistent Feeding**: A potassium-rich fertiliser once fruit sets will boost yields
4. **Don't Overwater**: Peppers tolerate dryness better than soggy roots
5. **Let Some Ripen Red**: Red jalapeños are sweeter with a different flavour profile

## Common Problems

- **Pale Leaves**: Often a nitrogen deficiency — feed with a balanced liquid fertiliser
- **Fruit Cracking**: Caused by irregular watering; mulch the base to retain moisture
- **Pepper Weevils**: Small holes in fruit; remove affected peppers immediately

## Harvest

Start picking when peppers are firm and have reached full size, even if still green. Harvesting frequently encourages the plant to set more fruit. Corking marks (tan lines on the skin) are normal and indicate the pepper was under stress — they tend to be hotter.
