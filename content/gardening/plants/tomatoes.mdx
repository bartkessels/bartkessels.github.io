Tomatoes are the crown jewel of my vegetable garden. Here's everything I've learned about growing them successfully in my climate.

## Varieties I Grow

### Roma (Paste Tomatoes)
Perfect for sauces and canning. Determinate variety that produces a heavy crop all at once.

### Cherry Tomatoes
My go-to snacking tomato. Indeterminate, so they produce all season long until frost.

## Growing Tips

1. **Start Early**: Begin seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before last frost
2. **Harden Off**: Gradually expose seedlings to outdoor conditions
3. **Deep Planting**: Bury the stem up to the first set of leaves
4. **Consistent Watering**: Irregular watering leads to blossom end rot
5. **Pruning**: Remove suckers on indeterminate varieties for larger fruit

## Common Problems

- **Blossom End Rot**: Caused by calcium deficiency, often from inconsistent watering
- **Hornworms**: Hand-pick these large caterpillars or use BT spray
- **Early Blight**: Remove affected leaves and improve air circulation

## Harvest

Pick when fully colored but still slightly firm. Tomatoes will continue to ripen on the counter.
