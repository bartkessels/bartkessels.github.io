Bell peppers are the mildest of the pepper family and one of the most versatile vegetables in the garden. They start green and sweeten as they ripen into red, yellow, or orange depending on the variety.

## Varieties I Grow

### California Wonder
The classic blocky bell pepper. Thick walls make it great for stuffing, and it turns a deep red when fully ripe.

### Sweet Banana
A longer, tapered variety that ripens from yellow to orange to red. Very prolific and a little sweeter than blocky types.

## Growing Tips

1. **Start Early Indoors**: Peppers need 8–10 weeks indoors before transplanting — longer than tomatoes
2. **Warmth is Essential**: Don't transplant until nights are reliably above 10°C (50°F)
3. **Stake Tall Plants**: Heavy fruit loads can snap branches in wind
4. **Even Moisture**: Inconsistent watering causes blossom drop
5. **Fertilise Carefully**: High nitrogen promotes leafy growth at the expense of fruit

## Common Problems

- **Blossom Drop**: Usually caused by temperature extremes or drought stress
- **Aphids**: Check the undersides of leaves; knock off with a strong water spray
- **Sunscald**: Pale papery patches on fruit from direct afternoon sun — provide light shade if needed

## Harvest

Pick green bells any time they reach full size. For coloured bells, leave them on the plant longer — flavour and vitamin C content increase significantly as they ripen. Use scissors or secateurs to avoid snapping branches.
